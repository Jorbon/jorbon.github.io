About Me

Hi! I'm a university student with a wide variety of technical and artistic hobbies. I study digital electronics, physics, and film and media in my degree program at the University of Kansas.

Some of the programming projects in my portfolio can be found on the Demos page of this site. Others are described in my project listings, including many that involve electronics hardware.

My primary career interest is to apply mathematics to technology to achieve visible, audible, or tangible impacts. To this end, I have experience with computer graphics, physics simulation, sound design, arranging and performing music, and programming lights for live shows.

This website is hosted on github pages, a static file-server platform. All of the HTML, scripting, and styling has been written by myself. The background animations use simple physics simulation techniques and are chosen because they have simple rules with interesting emergent behavior.
